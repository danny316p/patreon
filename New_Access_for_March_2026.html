New Access for March 2026
  • Everyone received access to a smattering of interesting tidbits throughout the month, primarily focusing on the ongoing household clean out project and ColonialLUG's collaborative Beach Town layout (the full display will actually include a boardwalk and beach with playable sandbox, but my creative contributions are limited to the town). One photo of my build process was featured in Flickr's Explore page this month. Other fun included a 5-photo preview of Ryan McNaught's Towers of Tomorrow exhibit, a quick trip to Hershey's Chocolate World (including a cake pickup), a very quick virtual WAMALUG-meeting Micropolis tile-inspired build, and a glance at another Inside Tour set.
    New public albums included ColonialLUG / SnailLUG members riffing on BioniLUG's Rat build challenge (3 photos) and Netflix House Philadelphia (53 photos), the latter of which is further split into Netflix House December 2025 (36 photos) and Netflix House February 2026 (17 photos).
  • Everyone on the Discord server received early access to two photos which still aren't public: one of Taipei 101 from Ryan McNaught's Towers of Tomorrow exhibit and one of the "Choisis & construis" wall at Le Magasin LEGO® Fairview Pointe Claire (au Québec, on parle français...)
  • Subscribers received access to dozens of bonus photos throughout the month, mostly covering early/bonus photos for Teddy Bear Tuesdays and the ColonialLUG 2026 Beach Town Build. One photo of Ryan McNaught's Towers of Tomorrow exhibit was exclusive to the #subscriber-media Discord channel. For new photos to close out the month, there are 223 photos from the SnailLUG March 2026 meeting at the AACA Library & Research Center. Some of these include pre-production artifacts from classic LEGO/McDonalds Happy Meal promotions courtesy of the meeting's host, Matt Hocker.
  • Members' early/bonus photos were mostly more of the same, with the exception of snapshots of an autographed LEGO House exclusive set that I've arranged to acquire. Two new albums of non-public photos are available, although one also contains everything in the other: The Museum After Dark – Makers Night Out (121 photos) and ColonialLUG at The Museum After Dark – Makers Night Out (94 photos). These are from an evening event at the Reading Public Museum, which is also hosting Ryan McNaught's Towers of Tomorrow exhibit.
  • Directors received access on March 13th to 68 photos from America on Wheels in February 2026.
  • ...and don't forget that you (everyone, for free) can follow my progress on build projects and organizing various collectibles, memorabilia, and historical items.
